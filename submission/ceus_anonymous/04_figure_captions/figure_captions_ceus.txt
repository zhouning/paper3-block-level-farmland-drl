Figure captions

Figure 1. Block-level planning framework. The DRL scheduler allocates budget across blocks, and a deterministic greedy engine executes parcel-level swaps within selected blocks.

Figure 2. Block construction on synthetic parcels resembling Township A: swappable parcels are separated by barriers, decomposed into connected components, subdivided by constrained clustering, and labeled as final blocks.

Figure 3. Training curves for Townships A-C, showing 5 seeds with a 200-episode moving average. Rewards stabilize by approximately 50K timesteps.

Figure 4. Cross-township comparison across slope, baimu fang area, contiguity, and baimu fang count. DRL is outlined in black.

Figure 5. Township B trajectory comparison between DRL (seed 0) and Reward-Greedy. Reward-Greedy gains slope but loses baimu fang area, whereas DRL preserves and slightly grows it.

Figure 6. Pareto analysis of slope reduction and baimu fang area change. DRL is the only primary method with positive baimu fang area on Township B.

Figure 7. Pareto analysis of slope reduction and baimu fang count change. DRL and Reward-Greedy satisfy both non-negative count and negative slope across all townships.

Figure 8. Township C trajectory of the DRL agent. The policy first forms five new baimu fang, then recovers slope while holding the count fixed.
